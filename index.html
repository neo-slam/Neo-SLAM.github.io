SLAM in Mobile-Excited Spatiotemporal Fields with Neural Operators

Paper Code for BKNO

Abstract

This paper presents a new paradigm of indoor simultaneous localization and mapping (SLAM) that operates in the spatiotemporal fields excited by a commodity mobile device and engaged with the environment. It is applicable to active sensors such as active sonar and mmWave radar. Its core is a neural operator pretrained to capture the general mapping from the composite function space of the mobile-emitted excitation and environment medium distribution to that of the excited spatiotemporal signal field. With this new construct, we advance SLAM from using a measurement map that only records the mobile's observations along the movement trajectory, to using a map engine that consists of the pretrained neural operator and the target environment's medium distribution updated by the SLAM algorithm. The map engine expands the mobile's understanding about the environment to the entire space, on par with the geometric maps produced by visual SLAM. This paper addresses two specific challenges in neural operator-based mapping: one on stability of the loop of pose estimate and medium distribution update, and the other on edge-native acceleration of the neural operator for practical real-time use. We achieve nearly 12$\times$ acceleration on edge CPU with only 6.8 MB memory footprint and sub-50 cm median localization error for two sensing modalities in five environments.
